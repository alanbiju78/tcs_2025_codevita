Detective Chu
Problem Description
Detective Pikachu, also known as Chu, was kidnapped during an investigation. Blindfolded, Chu was taken deep into the enemy's hideout.

During the journey, Chu was unconscious but woke up while still in transit. Although blindfolded, Chu used his sharp memory to track the vehicle's movements like every left turn (L), right turn (R), and straight movement (S).

Chu knows the city map, which is a grid made up of open cells (.) and obstacles (#). The enemy's vehicle could only travel through open cells. For each "S" (straight), the vehicle moved forward to the next cell in its current direction. For "L" (left) or "R" (right), the vehicle only changed direction while staying in the same cell.

Help Chu find all possible current locations based on the following information:

The city grid where (0, 0) is at top left and (R -1, C-1) is at bottom right
The movement sequence (a string of L, R, S)
The assumption that the vehicle could have started from any open cell, facing any direction (up, down, left, or right)
There is also a possibility that Chu was taken to a different city, in which case there will be no matches.
Constraints
3 <= R, C <= 50

2 <= Length of movement sequence <= 25

Input
The first line contains two integers, R and C, representing the number of rows and columns in the map.

The next R lines each contain C characters, either "." (open cell) or "#" (obstacle), representing the city map.

The last line contains the movement sequence, consisting of characters from ["S", "R", "L"].

Output
Output a single integer: the total number of possible locations where Chu could be, if at least one match exists.

If there are no possible locations, print "Impossible" (without quotes).

Time Limit (secs)
1

#.#.#

4

Explanation

The four possible locations he could be at are as given below.

(3, 3) assuming he started memorizing sequence at (0,4) cell going downwards.

(1, 2) assuming he started memorizing sequence at (4,1) cell going upwards.

(1, 4) assuming he started memorizing sequence at (4,3) cell going upwards.

Obstacles: Rocks (#) are obstacles. You cannot stand on them, but you can use them to climb upward into an available cell.
Moves: You may move left or right to adjacent treasure cells, or climb upward, following the rules. Each move, slide, or climb counts toward your maximum step limit, K. Climbing onto rocks does not count as a move.

$ -> Pearl
% -> Gold
* -> Platinum

Start from the source cell and reach a stable treasure cell within at most K steps. The last row is always stable (no gravity applies), but you cannot end your journey on a cell in the last row.

1 <= N, M <= 20

1 <= K <= 20

The index of top left cell is (0,0) and the bottom right cell is (N-1, M-1).

The starting cell is always stable.
